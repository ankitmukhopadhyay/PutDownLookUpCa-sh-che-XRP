🔗What is Blockchain? (The Super Simple Version)

Imagine a notebook that:
  • Everyone in the group has a copy of
  • Nobody can erase or change old pages
  • Everyone can read what's written
  • New pages are added automatically

That's a blockchain! It's like a public, permanent, copycat notebook that records everything.

Why is this useful?

✓ No lying: Once something is written down, nobody can change it. So nobody can say "I didn't promise to show up!"
✓ No forgetting: The notebook keeps EVERYTHING forever. You can look back and see all your past promises.
✓ No cheating: Since everyone has a copy, if one person tries to change their copy, everyone else sees it's a fake.

🤝How PUT UP Works: The Promise Game

Imagine Alice and Bob want to hang out at game night.

The Deal:

"If you don't show up, I get to keep your money."

Both friends put $10 into a "Bag" (like a pot). If both show up, they each get their $10 back. If one person ghosts, the other person gets their money.

The Steps:

1️⃣ Make the Promise

Alice and Bob both say "I promise to show up at game night" and put $10 into a shared pot on the blockchain.

2️⃣ Time to Show Up

Game night arrives. Alice and Bob check in using their phones (GPS or something similar proves they're there).

3️⃣ The Results

✓ Both showed up: Both get $10 back (promise kept!) + +10 Karma points
✗ Bob ghosted: Alice gets $10 + gets Bob's $10 + +15 Karma. Bob loses $10 and gets -20 Karma
✗ Both ghosted: Nobody wins, platform keeps both bags

4️⃣ Record It Forever

Everything is written in the blockchain notebook. Nobody can change it later or claim it didn't happen.

💎The Two Magic Layers

PUT UP has two separate tracking systems on the blockchain:

Layer 1: XRP (The Money)

🪙 Real Money on the Blockchain

This tracks the actual dollars people put in and take out. It's like the bank account for the Bags.

Example: Alice puts in $10 → System records this on blockchain → If she shows up, blockchain transfers it around based on the outcome

Layer 2: KRM (Karma Points)

⭐ Reputation Points

This is like a "grade" for how reliable you are. It's recorded on the blockchain forever so people can see how trustworthy you are.

Example: Alice shows up → Gets +10 KRM → Everyone can see "Alice has 50 KRM" which means "Alice is reliable"

Why Two Layers?

Money layer (XRP): Keeps you honest about whether you ACTUALLY show up (financial consequences)
Reputation layer (KRM): Keeps a permanent record of how reliable you are (social consequences)

📊The XRP Ledger: What You Saw in the Screenshot

The screenshot you showed me is called the "XRPL Explorer" — it's like a live view of a giant record book.

What's Happening in That Screenshot?

The Network Stats (top): Shows how fast the blockchain is operating
  • 15 Quorum: 15 computers agree on what's real (majority rule)
  • 0.005278 XRP avg fee: Costs almost nothing to record something
  • 3.910 sec interval: New "page" in the notebook every ~4 seconds
  • 41.32 TXN/SEC: About 41 transactions per second happening
The Color Legend: Shows different types of transactions
  • 🟢 GREEN = Payments (money moving)
  • 🔵 BLUE = DEX/Tokens (KRM karma points)
  • 🔴 RED = Account Changes (trust lines, settings)
  • ⭐ OTHERS (weird/special transactions)
The Blocks (bottom boxes): These are "pages" in the notebook
  • Each numbered box (21720815, 21720814, etc.) is a "ledger" — like one page
  • Each page has ~100-150 transactions on it
  • Every page is dated and numbered in order
  • The colored dots show what TYPE of transactions are on that page
  • You can click any transaction to see EXACTLY what happened

🎬The Quick Version (TLDR)

What is PUT UP?
A social commitment platform that uses money (escrow) to make friends keep their promises.
How does it work?
Alice and Bob put $10 in a pot → They promise to show up → GPS checks if they show up → Winner gets money → It's all recorded forever
What's the blockchain?
A permanent, public, unforgeable notebook that records everything forever. Nobody can lie about what happened.
What's XRPL?
The specific blockchain we're using. It's fast, cheap, and designed for tracking money and tokens.
What are KRM and XRP?
XRP = the actual money (like dollars on the blockchain)
KRM = reputation points (like a grade card showing how reliable you are)
What's the website for?
To look up anyone's karma score and see their full history of promises kept or broken. It's public and permanent!
Why does this matter?
Because when someone has a public record of ghosting, they can't just pretend it didn't happen. And when someone has a record of always showing up, people trust them more. This makes the world a little bit better because friends are less likely to flake on each other.

The Bottom Line

PUT UP uses blockchain to keep people accountable.

When you commit to showing up, it's recorded forever on a public ledger that nobody can change. You earn reputation points (KARMA) for being reliable, and you lose them for ghosting. It's as simple as that.

Everyone can see your track record. Nobody can lie. Friends actually show up.
